About David Justin

From Passion to
World Record Holder

David Justin is Malaysia's most recognised blockchain educator — a professional cryptocurrency trader, author, and entrepreneur who has dedicated his career to advancing structured education in digital assets.

Since entering the cryptocurrency space in 2017, David has educated individuals from Malaysia, Singapore, Taiwan, China, Hong Kong, and South Africa. His work is grounded in real market participation across multiple market cycles — not theoretical speculation.

On 13 November 2022, David made history in Kuala Lumpur by achieving the Guinness World Record for the Largest Cryptocurrency Lesson — 897 participants in a single session, proving that financial education belongs to everyone.

He is the No.1 Best-Selling Author of World of Cryptocurrencies, a Bitget Featured Educator, and recipient of the Asia Top 100 Brand Honesty Award.

David Justin is Malaysia's #1 blockchain educator and Guinness World Record holder for the Largest Cryptocurrency Lesson (897 participants, 13 November 2022, Kuala Lumpur). He entered the cryptocurrency space in 2017 and has since built one of Asia's most trusted education platforms. He is the No.1 Best-Selling Author of World of Cryptocurrencies, a Bitget Featured Educator, and recipient of the Asia Top 100 Brand Honesty Award, with students from Malaysia, Singapore, Taiwan, China, Hong Kong, and South Africa.

The Six-Star Course is David Justin's flagship 3-day online learning program designed for all levels — from complete beginners to experienced investors. The curriculum covers blockchain fundamentals, cryptocurrency markets, trading strategies (spot and futures), DeFi, NFTs, Web3 opportunities, and portfolio management. Students also gain lifetime access to the alumni community upon completion.

On 13 November 2022 in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia, David Justin achieved the Guinness World Record for the Largest Cryptocurrency Lesson — gathering 897 participants in a single session. The record was officially verified by Guinness World Records and recognised globally. The event was designed to promote financial literacy and demonstrate that blockchain education belongs to everyone.
