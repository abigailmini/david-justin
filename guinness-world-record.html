02 The story

Why 897, and why three days.

Blockchain education in Southeast Asia was — and largely still is — fragmented. Retail learners chase price charts. University curricula lag two cycles behind the technology. Enterprises treat the entire category as a compliance checkbox.

The Largest Blockchain Lesson was designed as a single, public answer to that gap: a rigorously prepared, three-day lesson delivered to 897 participants in a single class — not a conference, not a panel, not a series of overlapping workshops. One instructor, one syllabus, one hall, one record.

Why 897? Because that was every verified seat filled across all three days. Guinness required the same registered learners to be present at the start and end of each day for the count to hold. Nobody was a walk-in. Nobody was a replacement.

Hosted at MITEC — Malaysia International Trade & Exhibition Centre in Kuala Lumpur over 13–15 November 2022, the attempt was independently witnessed throughout by a Guinness World Records adjudicator. The record stands today as the world's largest verified single-instructor blockchain lesson.

For David Justin personally, it was the beginning of a much larger mission — to make this technology accessible to one million learners across the region.

03 Verification

How Guinness verified it.

A Guinness World Records title is not self-awarded. Every attempt must meet a pre-approved set of criteria, witnessed independently and evidenced contemporaneously. Here is how we met each requirement.

  1. 01
    Pre-approved category
    Application submitted in advance; Guinness reviewed the proposed lesson topic, duration, and participant definition before accepting the attempt.
  2. 02
    Single instructor, single topic
    One lead educator (David Justin) delivered the entire three-day lesson on a single subject — blockchain fundamentals — with no rotating speakers or co-presenters.
  3. 03
    Minimum qualifying duration
    Each day's session ran for the required uninterrupted duration, monitored on an official timekeeper's clock across all three days.
  4. 04
    Participant identification & attendance
    All 897 participants registered individually, were physically present at the start and end of each day of the lesson, and were verified by two independent witnesses.
  5. 05
    Independent adjudication
    A Guinness World Records adjudicator was present in the venue for the full duration, confirming compliance with every published guideline before announcing the result.
